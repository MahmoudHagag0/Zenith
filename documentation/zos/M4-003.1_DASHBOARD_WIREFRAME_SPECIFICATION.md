# M4-003.1_DASHBOARD_WIREFRAME_SPECIFICATION

**Document ID:** ZOS-M4-003.1
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Status:** Proposed — Awaiting Product Leadership Review
**Owner:** Architecture Team (leadership-authored philosophy; engineering-maintained specification)
**Milestone:** M4 — Design Foundation (Dashboard Design phase)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Purpose

The low-fidelity structural wireframe for Dashboard, built from
`M4-003`'s Information Architecture — layout, hierarchy, region, and
container structure only. **No color, no icons, no visual polish, no
component skins.** This document also records the UX/behavioral review
performed against the initial draft of this wireframe and the Psychology
Validation performed against the resulting final layout, per this
task's own workflow — both are recorded here, against the same
artifact, rather than as separate documents, since neither review makes
sense read apart from the layout it critiques.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. UX & Behavioral Review

Reviewed against Cognitive Psychology, HCI, Behavioral Economics,
Trading Psychology, Decision Fatigue, Eye Tracking, Working Memory,
Information Density, Visual Hierarchy, and Long-Session Usability, per
this task's own required review lenses. Each finding states whether a
genuine weakness exists or whether the draft already satisfies the
governing rule — consistent with this project's own standing discipline
of not inventing problems where none exist.

## 2.1 Eye Tracking / Visual Hierarchy — Primary region placement

**Reviewed:** does the Primary region (`DASH-002`) sit inside the
highest-attention zone research identifies (D1-001 §2, Nielsen F/Z-
pattern)?

**Finding:** the initial draft already places it first, full-width,
immediately below navigation — the top-left/top-band region eye-tracking
research identifies as highest-attention. **No weakness found; already
correct.**

## 2.2 Visual Hierarchy — Supporting region competing with Primary

**Reviewed:** does `DASH-003` (Confidence & Uncertainty Disclosure), as
drawn, risk reading as a second, competing region rather than
subordinate content?

**Finding — genuine weakness.** The initial draft renders `DASH-003` as
its own full-width bordered container directly beneath `DASH-002`'s own
container, at equal visual rank. Two adjacent, equally-bordered regions
invite a trader to treat them as two things to look at, rather than one
synthesized reading with its own attached disclosure — this is exactly
ZXL §4.2's "competition between components... forcing the trader to
adjudicate between them instead of the product having already done so."

**Fix applied:** `DASH-003` is nested *inside* `DASH-002`'s own region
boundary (no separate container, no separate border) — structurally
subordinate, per ZXL §1.3 ("it discloses, it never competes"). See §3
below.

## 2.3 Working Memory / Information Density

**Reviewed:** do `DASH-004`, `DASH-005`, and `DASH-006`, shown
simultaneously, exceed the active-reasoning ceiling of ~4 chunks (D1-001
§1, Cowan 2001; ZXL §3.1)?

**Finding:** no genuine weakness. The ceiling applies to *actively-
reasoned* information, not raw visible-element count (ZXL §3.1
explicitly). `DASH-002` is the only element a trader actively reasons
about; `DASH-004`–`DASH-006` are Secondary-Attention entry points,
consulted rather than held in mind simultaneously (ZXL §1.2) — a trader
scans three labeled bridges to other screens, which is a recognition
task, not a working-memory-load task. **Already correct — same
disposition as the Stress Budget finding in the D1 pre-merge review;
stated here rather than silently assumed.**

## 2.4 Behavioral Economics / Trading Psychology — Watchlist vs. Portfolio ordering

**Reviewed:** `26` §3 (`DASH-006`) left this ordering an open `TODO`:
"ordering between DASH-005 and DASH-006 relative to each other is
`TODO` (neither Constitution nor Blueprint states which of Watchlist or
Portfolio ranks higher on Dashboard specifically)."

**Finding — genuine, previously-unresolved question, resolved here.**
Portfolio concerns capital already at risk; Watchlist concerns
instruments merely being tracked. Constitution §9.3 names Decision as
"the highest-stakes, most emotionally-loaded moment," and `M4-002.2`
§2.7 names Trade Monitoring (Portfolio's own moment) "the disposition-
effect danger zone." A professional trader's first "where do things
stand" concern, immediately after Decision Readiness, is what is
already committed (held capital) before what is merely being watched.

**Fix applied:** Portfolio Snapshot (`DASH-006`) is ordered before
Watchlist Snapshot (`DASH-005`) in reading order (left-to-right at
`breakpoint.wide`, top-to-bottom at `breakpoint.compact`) — resolving
`26`'s own open `TODO` with a stated rationale rather than leaving it to
implementation-time chance. This does not alter either component's own
specification in `26` — only their relative position in this wireframe.

## 2.5 Decision Fatigue / Long-Session Usability

**Reviewed:** does the layout impose a different or heavier
re-orientation cost on a trader's fifth Dashboard visit of the day than
their first (Constitution §6.2)?

**Finding:** no weakness. Nothing in the layout is state-dependent in
structure — Loading/Empty/Error/Success (`26` §3, per component) all
occupy the identical region and footprint; only content differs, never
position (Design Constitution §8.1). A trader re-visiting at hour 8
re-encounters exactly the same structural landmarks as at hour 0.
**Already correct.**

## 2.6 Information Density — Placeholder block (`DASH-007`–`DASH-011`)

**Reviewed:** `26` §5, Engineering Observation 5, flagged as an open
product decision: "whether V1 shows Placeholder components at all,"
weighing cognitive-load cost (Constitution §6.1, Product Rule 3) against
future-discoverability benefit.

**Finding — genuine weakness in the initial draft.** Five separate,
individually-bordered inactive cards at Peripheral attention (§2.3
distinguishes Peripheral from Secondary, but five simultaneous bordered
regions still add visible complexity a trader's eye must at least
parse-and-dismiss on every visit) sit below four already-substantive P0
blocks — pushing total simultaneous regions to nine on a screen whose
own governing density tier is Low (D1-002 §4.4, Synthesis Archetype).
This also under-serves the Learning Loop framing this task requires:
five undifferentiated inactive cards do not read as "the rest of your
Learning Loop is coming," they read as clutter.

**Fix applied:** the five Placeholder entries are consolidated into
**one** compact, low-weight Peripheral affordance — a single row of
five small, equally-styled inactive labels (not five separate
component-shaped cards) — resolving `26`'s own Observation 5 in favor of
showing the areas (preserving discoverability and the Learning-Loop-
in-progress signal) while keeping their total visual footprint and
region count to one Peripheral element, not five. This changes only
*presentation density* in this wireframe — `DASH-007` through
`DASH-011` remain exactly the components `26` defines, each still
individually reachable and replaced (not merely relabeled) once its own
area ships (`26` §3, Future Extensibility, per component).

## 2.7 Cognitive Psychology — Loading state and session-start anxiety

**Reviewed:** `26` §5, Engineering Observation 1, flags `DASH-002`'s own
aggregation cost as the single most expensive query on the page, with a
possibly-long visible Loading State at the exact moment (session start)
a trader's context is lowest and stakes-sensitivity is arguably highest.

**Finding:** already governed, not a new gap. `26` §3 (`DASH-002`,
Loading State) already specifies a skeleton reflecting the component's
own eventual shape, never a generic full-page spinner; D2-005 §17
(Skeleton) already mandates a slow, low-contrast pulse, never a bright
shimmer. This wireframe adds no new rule — it confirms the Loading
State occupies the identical Primary-region footprint as the eventual
Success State (§2.5 above), so the trader's own eye does not need to
relocate anything once data arrives.

is unchanged; only the three Secondary-Attention *entry points'* own
left-to-right reading order within Dashboard's Secondary row is
addressed here (§2.4), which is a wireframe-level decision this
document is authorized to make, not a Decision Flow re-ordering, which
it is not.

# 5. Psychology Validation

Per this task's own required test, every block in the final wireframe
(§3) is validated against all five questions. A block that fails any
question is removed; none below fails.

## `DASH-002` (Primary Region, incl. nested `DASH-003`)

1. **Does it help the trader?** Yes — it is the single fastest path to
   "am I decision-ready," eliminating manual cross-referencing
   (Constitution §10.2 Success Criteria).
2. **Why does it exist?** To answer Decision Flow Q1 in one glance
   (ZXL §2) — not decorative; removing it removes Dashboard's entire
   purpose.
3. **Behavioral influence:** *Emotional* — oriented, calm (D1-005 §1.1).
   *Cognitive* — collapses N instruments' worth of evidence into one
   synthesized read, reducing extraneous load (Constitution §12.2).
   *Behavioral* — nothing about it prompts an action; it informs.
4. **Reinforces Zenith philosophy?** Yes — makes a better-informed
   decision the default, easiest path; the paired uncertainty disclosure
   (nested `DASH-003`) directly reduces overconfidence (ZXL §7.4).
5. **Learning Loop stage(s):** Understand, Decide (`M4-003` §9) —
   because it is literally the Confluence Engine's synthesis disclosed
   before any chart, the definition of Understand at `M4-002.2`'s
   Learning Loop §3.

## `DASH-004` Morning Brief Entry

1. **Helps the trader?** Yes — bridges to the day's own full synthesis
   without forcing a second, separate search for it.
2. **Why does it exist?** Dashboard is the hub; every area needs an
   entry (`26` §3).
3. **Behavioral influence:** *Emotional* — briefed, prepared (D1-005
   §1.1). *Cognitive* — a one-line preview, not a full copy, so no
   duplicated reading burden. *Behavioral* — a single navigation action.
4. **Reinforces philosophy?** Yes — supports Story Before Chart
   (Constitution §12.1) by pointing to where that discipline is fully
   realized.
5. **Learning Loop stage(s):** Observe, Understand (`M4-003` §9).

## `DASH-006` Portfolio Snapshot

1. **Helps the trader?** Yes — a calm, neutral view of real capital's
   own current state, without a separate navigation.
2. **Why does it exist?** Portfolio is a stated Dashboard Exit Point
   (`25_PRODUCT_BLUEPRINT.md` §2).
3. **Behavioral influence:** *Emotional* — steady, confident regardless
   of favorability (Constitution §7.3). *Cognitive* — low; a neutral
   summary, not a full ledger. *Behavioral* — directly counters the
   disposition effect by never dramatizing a loss relative to a gain.
4. **Reinforces philosophy?** Yes — identical-treatment-regardless-of-
   favorability is a direct discipline mechanism (Constitution §7.3,
   §12.4).
5. **Learning Loop stage(s):** Observe, and a forward bridge to Record
   (`M4-003` §9).

## `DASH-005` Watchlist Snapshot

1. **Helps the trader?** Yes — confirms tracked-instrument status
   without a separate navigation.
2. **Why does it exist?** Watchlist is a stated Dashboard Exit Point
   (`25_PRODUCT_BLUEPRINT.md` §2).
3. **Behavioral influence:** *Emotional* — in control, focused (D1-005
   §1.1). *Cognitive* — a bounded, intentional set, never an open feed
   (D1-005 §2.2). *Behavioral* — reinforces intentional attention
   allocation over compulsive unbounded checking (Constitution §6.4).
4. **Reinforces philosophy?** Yes — a bounded set is itself an
   anti-compulsion design decision.
5. **Learning Loop stage(s):** Observe (`M4-003` §9).

## Peripheral row (`DASH-007`–`DASH-011`, consolidated)

1. **Helps the trader?** Yes, narrowly — discloses honestly that the
   full Learning Loop (Record/Reflect/Learn, once Journal and AI
   Workspace ship) exists and will be reachable from here, without
   claiming those stages are already served.
2. **Why does it exist?** Preserves the navigation-hub relationship
   (`26` §2) without fabricating functional content ahead of each area's
   own backend (`26` §5, Observation 5, resolved §2.6 above).
3. **Behavioral influence:** *Emotional* — none intended; deliberately
   low-weight so it does not compete with or worry the trader about
   "missing" inactive areas. *Cognitive* — minimal, one compact row.
   *Behavioral* — none; entries are inactive.
4. **Reinforces philosophy?** Yes — honesty about what does not yet
   exist is itself Constitution §5.3 (Trust Through Transparency)
   applied to product scope, not only to evidence.
5. **Learning Loop stage(s):** Record, Reflect, Learn, Improve (future,
   once built) — disclosed as *pointed-to*, not performed, per `M4-003`
   §9's own honesty discipline for `DASH-007`/`DASH-008`.

No block in the final wireframe fails any of the five questions; none
is removed.
